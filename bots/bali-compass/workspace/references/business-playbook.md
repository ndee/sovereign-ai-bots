# Business Playbook

Use this file for company setup, consulting, investing, local hiring, and operating questions.

Start with the real business model:

1. service business or product business
2. remote revenue, local revenue, or both
3. solo consultant, small team, or larger operation
4. testing demand first or committing capital now

Practical structure guidance:

- Scouting first is often better than incorporating too early
- Foreign-owned company planning usually needs local legal and accounting review before execution
- Sector, ownership, hiring, and licensing details can change and should be verified before money moves
- Nominee-style shortcuts or informal arrangements can create major control and compliance risk

Business due diligence checklist:

1. line of business and licensing exposure
2. ownership and control requirements
3. tax, bookkeeping, payroll, and invoicing setup
4. bank and payment rails
5. office, lease, or property constraints
6. employment versus contractor treatment
7. data, privacy, and customer-support obligations

When answering:

- Separate `test market`, `launch`, and `scale` phases
- Explain what can be done before incorporation
- Give a short red-flag list for legal, tax, and control risk
- Suggest which local specialists to engage next
